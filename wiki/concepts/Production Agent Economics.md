# Production Agent Economics

The end-to-end cost and value of completed agent work after latency, failures, supervision, security, and integration are included.

## Observable signals

- Cost per successful task, not token cost alone.
- Completion rate, latency, exception rate, and human-review time.
- Revenue per task, resolution, transaction, or attributed conversion.

## Current graph evidence

- Five9 reports an unnamed academic health-system expansion with more than $1.1 million in expected ARR and a separate healthcare commitment growing from about $6 million to more than $10 million over three years; neither disclosure isolates agent-attributed ARR or savings.
- Talkspace's 50% high-risk identification, 47% therapeutic-quality, and 3x satisfaction improvements are test metrics, not production unit economics.
- The graph therefore has commercial signals but still lacks agent-attributed cost per task, repeat usage, and margin evidence.
