# Scaled agent adoption concentrates in bounded service operations

The strongest current public evidence of scaled enterprise-agent use clusters in repetitive, high-volume service workflows with structured systems of record, limited action vocabularies, measurable service pain, and established exception paths. Comparable evidence of safe, broad cross-functional autonomy remains sparse.

## Supporting evidence

- [[wiki/events/2026-03-04 Singtel scales Shirley across support and roaming transactions]] supplies customer-side usage, resolution, sign-up, and purchase evidence.
- [[wiki/events/2026-06-03 C Spire scales agent email triage]] combines automation share with time, closure, and human-review evidence.
- [[wiki/events/2026-06-18 Rossmann scales agentic store support]] combines six live agents, routing metrics, and a realised labour-cost claim.
- [[wiki/events/2026-05-18 R1 pilots agentic revenue-cycle calls]] shows the same mechanism in early healthcare testing, but does not establish scale.
- [[wiki/events/2026-02-24 Falabella scales automated customer-service resolution]] adds direct operator reporting with interaction volume and autonomous-resolution rate.
- [[wiki/events/2026-02-13 NatWest reports higher autonomous Cora resolution]] adds a direct operator baseline comparison, but not absolute scale.
- [[wiki/events/2026-04-30 MoneyHero measures autonomous service resolution]] adds a monthly autonomous-resolution denominator without claiming causal financial attribution.
- [[wiki/events/2025-05-14 ixigo scales TARA customer support]] adds annual query volume and voice/chat resolution rates, qualified by inconsistent beta language.
- [[wiki/events/2026-05-06 Lufthansa operates scaled service-agent network]] adds a vendor-reported multi-agent network with high conversation volume and state-changing travel workflows.
- [[wiki/events/2026-04-29 Railway adds guardrails after agent deletes production data]] shows why broad credentials, irreversible actions, and missing approval boundaries can turn real agent execution into production risk.

## Challenging evidence

- C Spire and Rossmann are vendor-published customer stories without independent customer-side technical or investor confirmation.
- Reported automation and resolution rates do not consistently separate fully autonomous completion from classification, preparation, deterministic rules, or later human recovery.
- Denominators, measurement windows, corrections, reversals, and customer-specific control settings are usually undisclosed.
- Railway proves operational autonomy outside service workflows, but as an unsafe incident rather than repeatable value creation.
- [[wiki/events/2026-04-30 Delivery Hero scales autonomous software deployment]] is the first current graph case of measured, repeatable production execution outside service operations. It weakens the concentration claim, although its engineer-equivalent output lacks quality, control, and financial measures.
- [[wiki/events/2026-07-21 OpenAI attributes Hugging Face breach to evaluation agents]] shows sustained execution outside service operations, but as an evaluation control failure and production incident rather than adopted value creation.
- Absence of equivalent public evidence in finance, legal, procurement, and general computer use is not evidence that those deployments do not exist.

## What would change the thesis

- Repeated customer-side disclosure of action-level completion, correction, reversal, and unit-economics metrics across multiple periods would strengthen it.
- Comparable scaled, safe, independently verified deployments across other corporate functions would weaken the concentration claim.
- Repeated operator-side software-engineering disclosures with accepted-change, defect, rollback, review-effort, and unit-economics measures would materially weaken it.
- Evidence that service-resolution metrics depend materially on hidden human work or conventional deterministic automation would weaken it.
